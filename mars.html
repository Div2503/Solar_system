Mars
  • Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun and the second smallest planet in the Solar System.
  • The red-orange appearance of the Martian surface is caused by ferric oxide, or rust.
  • It includes Olympus Mons, the largest volcano and highest-known mountain in the Solar System.
  • It also has Valles Marineris, one of the largest canyons in the Solar System.
  • Mars has two small, irregularly shaped moons, Phobos and Deimos.
  • It lost its magnetosphere 4 billion years ago, possibly because of numerous asteroid strikes.
  • The lengths of the its seasons are twice those of Earth's because of its greater distance from the Sun.
  • Its surface temperatures vary from lows of about −110 °C to highs of up to 35 °C.
  • Martian soil is slightly alkaline and contains elements like magnesium, sodium, potassium and chlorine.
  • The solar wind interacts directly with its ionosphere, lowering its atmospheric density.
