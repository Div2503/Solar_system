ISS
  • Sputnik 1 was the first artificial Earth satellite.
  • It was launched into an elliptical low Earth orbit by the Soviet Union on 4 October 1957 as part of the Soviet space program.
  • The satellite traveled at a peak speed of about 8 km/s (18,000 mph), taking 96.20 minutes to complete each orbit.
  • The R-7 rocket was initially designed as an intercontinental ballistic missile (ICBM) by OKB-1.
  • it was made by the Central Committee of the Communist Party of the Soviet Union and the Council of Ministers of the USSR on 20 May 1954.
  • At least two vintage duplicates of Sputnik 1 exist, built apparently as backup units.
  • One resides just outside Moscow in the corporate museum of Energia, the modern descendant of Korolev's design bureau, where it is on display by appointment only. Another is in the Museum of Flight in Seattle, Washington.
